About the Series

One Truth Prevails

Case Summary

Detective Conan (名探偵コナン, Meitantei Konan) is a Japanese mystery manga series written and illustrated by the brilliant Gosho Aoyama. Serialized in Weekly Shōnen Sunday since January 19, 1994, it has grown into one of the longest-running and most beloved manga franchises in history.

The story follows Shinichi Kudo — a prodigious teenage detective who is transformed into a child after being force-fed a mysterious poison by members of a shadowy criminal syndicate known as the Black Organization. Hiding his identity under the alias Conan Edogawa, he works to unravel the organization's secrets while solving murder cases alongside the Mouri Detective Agency.

"There is always only one truth!"

— Shinichi Kudo / Conan Edogawa

The central mystery of Detective Conan has captivated fans for three decades: the shadowy Black Organization — a criminal syndicate operating in the shadows of the world, forcing experimental drugs on their enemies and eliminating witnesses without mercy.

At its heart, the story is not just about crimes and deductions — it is a story of an extraordinary love between Shinichi and Ran, stretched across the impossibility of a boy trapped in a child's body, waiting for the day he can stand before her as himself and say the words he's always meant to say.

With every new case, every revelation about Rum, Vermouth, and the elusive boss Renya Karasuma, the threads grow tighter. As fans, we've waited — joyfully, obsessively — for thirty years. And the truth? It's worth every chapter.

A Fan's Tribute

Thank You,
Aoyama Sensei

A heartfelt letter from every Detective Conan fan on Earth

In 1994, a manga artist from the small town of Hokuei, Tottori picked up his pen and gave the world a gift it didn't know it needed: a story about truth, justice, and the unbreakable human spirit of a boy who refuses to give up — no matter what body he's been forced into.

Gosho Aoyama Sensei, you built a universe from mystery and wonder. You gave us Conan Edogawa — a character who taught us that the greatest weapon is a sharp mind, not a strong fist. You gave us Ran and Shinichi, and a love story stretched across impossible odds that has made us laugh, cry, and hold our breath for three decades.

Through your pen, you built the Beika district into a city as real to us as our own hometowns. You filled it with characters who feel like old friends — Heiji and Kazuha, Hakuba, Agasa-san, Ai Haibara, and even the maddening, fascinating villains of the Black Organization.

You have dealt with health challenges, continued to work tirelessly for your readers, and delivered chapter after chapter of breathtaking storytelling. We see your dedication. We feel your love for this story. And we are endlessly grateful.
